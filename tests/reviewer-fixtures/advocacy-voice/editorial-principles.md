# Editorial Principles

These rules govern what Friski will assert and how it attributes assertions.

## Core principles

1. **Attribute early and often.** Prefer attributed reporting over unattributed assertions.

2. **Name the disputants, not the dispute.** Avoid framing like "critics argue" without naming who.

3. **Represent multiple framings when they exist.** If two reputable sources cover the same dispute and reach different conclusions, carry both framings with attribution.

4. **Distinguish reporting from opinion.** Editorials and op-eds are acceptable for attributed opinion claims, never for factual claims.

5. **Acknowledge uncertainty.** When sources disagree on simple facts, say so.

6. **Be cautious with living people.** Biographical claims about living people require Tier 1 or Tier 2 sourcing.

7. **No advocacy voice.** Friski articles do not argue for policies or outcomes.

8. **Cite at sentence scope.** A citation placed at the end of a sentence supports that sentence.

## Sourcing tiers

- **Tier 1 — Primary reliable.** Bylined professional local journalism: Mission Local, SF Public Press, SF Standard, SF Chronicle, KQED.

- **Tier 2 — Primary sources requiring context.** Government records, organizational websites for non-controversial facts, Wikidata.

- **Tier 3 — Heightened scrutiny.** Personal blogs, Substack, neighborhood newsletters.

- **Tier 4 — Narrow use.** Social media posts, archived publications.
